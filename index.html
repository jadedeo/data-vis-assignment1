Overall Analysis Questions

  1. What does this data look like as a function of time? Specifically, did COVID-19 have an effect on overdose rates? An increase in cases of depression, anxiety, and other mental health-related illnesses was noted under the stressful circumstances of the pandemic. I wonder if this trend would follow for alcohol and drug use.
  2. The drug epidemic and particularly the fentanyl crisis have been in the news for years. Will this data reflect that? How have the patterns of substance use in overdose deaths changed over time?
  3. What trends exist between various demographic characteristics and fatal overdoses? Who is most affected by substance overdoses?

Initial Overview

The Dataset

This dataset contains 7,000+ entries pertaining to substance overdose deaths in Allegheny county. This includes: death date and time, decedent's age, sex, race, the zip code in which the body was received (Incident Zip), and the substances associated with the overdose (Combined Od1 to 10).

Data Quality

The dataset includes many entries with zip codes that were clearly not in Pennsylvania, and many others still that are not in Allegheny county. Despite some refinement, those in the map are still not solely confined to the county. There were also a small number of records with the race of the decedent listed as “None” that needed to be filtered out; I wonder why these records were no classified under "U"/"Unknown".

Perhaps not an issue of quality, but I noticed that the data for 2024 seems drastically different and acknowledge that the year is not year complete (see Figure 3 and Figure 7). I wonder if this marked decrease is accurate and there truly have been such significant strides in reducing fatal substance overdoses, or whether this lends to that incompleteness.
