Overall Analysis Questions

I'm curious as to how outside forces, particularly global events, trigger changes in overdose trends.

  1. Before targeting those changes, I need to know who they're affecting. Coming from an exceedingly diverse city (NYC), I've always heard that substances disproportionately affect marginalized communities. What trends exist between various demographic characteristics and fatal overdoses? Who is most affected by substance overdoses? Where are these incidents happening?
  2. The drug epidemic and particularly the fentanyl crisis have been in the news for years. Will this data reflect that? How have the patterns of substance use in overdose deaths changed over time?

Initial Overview

The Dataset

This dataset contains 7,000+ entries pertaining to substance overdose deaths in Allegheny county. This includes: death date and time, decedent's age, sex, race, the zip code in which the body was received (Incident Zip), and the substances associated with the overdose (Combined Od1 to 10).

Data Quality

The dataset includes many entries with zip codes that were clearly not in Pennsylvania, and many others still that are not in Allegheny county. Despite some refinement, those in the map are still not solely confined to the county. There were also a small number of records with the race of the decedent listed as “None” that needed to be filtered out; I wonder why these records were not classified under "U"/"Unknown".

Perhaps not an issue of quality, but I noticed that the data for 2024 seems drastically different and acknowledge that the year is not yet complete (see Figure 3 and Figure 7). I wonder if this marked decrease is accurate and there truly have been such significant strides in reducing fatal substance overdoses, or whether this lends to that incompleteness.

Discoveries and Insights

Summary

This analysis has delved into the intricate dynamics of drug overdose trends within Allegheny County, serving as an initial look at how societal shifts have influenced patterns of substance overdoses. The exploration revealed significant demographic disparities in overdose occurrences, with infamous substances like Fentanyl markedly affecting various age groups and races. As mentioned in the captions, there are a few instances they could be further improved if joined with general census/demographic data, or additional information about the decedent. Evem so, the visualizations provide a comprehensive overview of the overdose landscape, from geographical hotspots to temporal trends, highlighting critical areas for intervention. The insights derived could serve to emphasize the necessity for targeted public health strategies that consider both the sociodemographic and spatial factors contributing to the drug epidemic. Understanding these trends is crucial for crafting effective policies and preventive measures to combat the ongoing crisis in drug-related fatalities.
